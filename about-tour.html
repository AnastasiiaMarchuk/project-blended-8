Tour description

The most beautiful palace and park complex in Crimea is located in the city of Alupka. This is probably the richest palace on the peninsula. Almost everything is impressive here - monumental buildings built in architectural styles that combine Moorish traditions characteristic of the peninsula and English motifs. The complex fits perfectly into the surrounding nature. The peaks of Mount Ai-Petri are visible from the territory of the palace. The rich decoration of the interior halls of the palace is striking. Each of them is designed in its own style. Definitely worth visiting this place in the summer when there are many flower beds around.

Route description

  • On this journey, you will have a New-reading of the seemingly well-known sights of the South Shore.

  • In the palace of Count Vorontsov you will find signs of belonging of the "transformer of Taurida" to the secret order "Foggy Alhambra".

  • In the park surrounding the palace, you will find out why Vorontsov decided to change the configuration of the earth's space and the sea surface.

  • The cable car to Ai-Petri will give you the opportunity to walk through the sky and try to unravel the mysteries of the patriarchal trees of the Crimean forest.

  • By visiting the Swallow's Nest castle, you will find out why its mistresses were exclusively women.

Importent

Dress code is comfortable. Hats, sun protection. Bring snacks and water with you. When visiting the Ai-Petri plateau, you must have warm clothes.

Important information

When crossing the border (Ukraine-Poland), you should have with you:

  • Ukrainian passport

  • Citizens under 14 years of age birth certificate

    Minor children cross the border accompanied by their parents, accompanied by a legal representative (notarized power of attorney indicating the country of export of the child).

You don't need a visa to cross the border, just an ID-card and $200 in cash

!

When returning from Poland to Ukraine, you do not need to take a PLR test, they will not be quarantined (Resolution of the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine 8676 of 08/01/2020).
